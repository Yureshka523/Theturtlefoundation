Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

A conservation initiative devoted to safeguarding sea turtles and their eggs is the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, which is situated near the coastal town of Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka. The hatchery is an essential part in preserving these gorgeous creatures. It was established to address the declining number of sea turtles caused by numerous threats.


Visitors to the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery have the opportunity to learn about the different species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka, including the Olive Ridley, Loggerhead, Hawksbill, and Green turtles. They can witness the various stages of a turtle's life, from egg incubation to the release of hatchlings into the sea. Education programs and guided tours are conducted to raise awareness about the importance of sea turtle conservation and the threats they face.
