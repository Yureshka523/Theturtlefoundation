KOSGODA TURTLE HATCHERY

FOUNDED BY

Dr. Upen de Silva and Dr. R. S. B Wickremsinghe founded the first turtle hatchery in Sri Lanka in 1978.

OUR LIFELONG COMMITMENT

is to make it simple to go green while also protecting and conserving Sri Lanka's wildlife and habitats, particularly endangered species.

The primary goal is to save nature for future generations by leading, facilitating, and encouraging humans to save, conserve, and improve the environment, including national habitats, flora, and fauna.

Motivate people to take conservation actions in any way they can to protect the flora, fauna, biodiversity, and natural environment.

HOW IT STARTED

This is Sri Lanka's first conservation and research project, which began in early 1978. It was at a time when all seven species of sea turtles were listed as Endangered.

Five of the seven species were discovered to be frequenting the beaches of Sri Lanka's southern coast to lay their eggs. The villagers collected these eggs and sold them on the open market.

OUR MISSION

Work for the conservation of sea turtle and extent the knowledge over the world.

OUR VISION

Save the sea turtle for the future.
